Geocluster Upload

An upload flow where the biggest risk wasn't a broken UI but users committing errors before they understood the rules. The challenge was deciding where the rules should live.

Original Design Draft
Designer's initial proposal
The Problem

The upload flow required users to follow strict formatting rules — timestamp formats, coordinate conventions, and naming constraints. Without them, files would fail or produce incorrect data.

The designer's initial design took the simpler path: links to download sample files and a link to the guidelines, keeping the modal clean. However, it meant that guidelines were in documentation that most users would never open before uploading.

Two risks framed the conversation. Hide the guidelines and risk users learning through repeated errors. Surface everything and risk overwhelming users before they even try. Neither option felt right. The exploration continued.

Why This Works

With the accordion blocked, one question settled the design: which risk is harder to recover from? Cognitive load is recoverable. Silent data errors from a malformed file are not.

Hence, we kept the guidelines but moved them below the upload zone. This was not the ideal hierarchy, but a deliberate tradeoff. Users who are confident can proceed immediately. Users who aren't have everything they need without leaving the modal.

A second filter shaped what made it in: only rules that directly cause errors. Edge cases went to documentation.

Two copy choices reinforced this logic. The sample file links lack the "Download" prefix; they appear as hyperlinks, and users expect them to download, which they do. Adding the verb would have loaded the screen further. Also, a part of the subtitle — "and guidelines to avoid errors" — signals that skipping them may have a consequence, without making it a requirement.
